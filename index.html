About Me

Who is this women? Dreamer, Creative or Super Designer...

My name is Thalia Bacilio and I am 25 years old. At first, I intended to be a publicist when I was studying advertising in Mexico; however, my studies were interrupted when I moved to Toronto. There, I worked as volunteer designing flayers, invitations, logos, brochures for my Christian church PIAH for almost three years. It was then that I realized that I wanted to become a graphic designer and to have my own company. A company that shares the word of God (Holy Bible) through innovative design. Later, I got married, moved to Ottawa and started my graphic design program at Algonquin College. Meanwhile I am studying, I am currently available for freelance work. I specialize in Print Design, Photography & Branding, so feel free to contact me at any time on any issue such as:

  • "I want to increase the quality of my brand."
  • "I need some promotional material (business cards, flyers, postcards, labels,etc)."
  • "I want unique concepts for my company."
  • "I am stressed out from not having creative ideas."

Portfolio

Below are a few of my favorite projects, both personal and professional. If you think I would be a good match for your next project, contact me and I will get back to you as soon as possible.

Chana's Logo

The Chana's flavors logo portrays the spirit of the mexican cuisine. The logo has icons of the main elements that season mexican food such as: tomato, onion, jalapeños and pepper. These icons are represented by white lines inside squares. Due to the fact that Mexico is well known by it energetic colours, the colour palette is formed by vibrant hues such as; red, pink, turquoise and green. Sans serif and handwriting (script) font have been used to represent the name of the business.

iPhone Skin Design

The theme for this iPhone skin design is Dreams in Paris. The scene is formed by a women with a chic outfit holding balloons near the Eiffel Tower. In addition, there are some buildings on the background and a colourful strip on the bottom (floor). An analogous colour palette (purple, blue and green) has been used to give visual harmony. Effects like Mesh Gradient, Sponge Effect, Gaussian Blur, Distort Glass and Ink brush have been used to give texture and dynamism to the design.

Cover Book

Due to the fact that one of the main points on the book is to fulfil Ambrose's dream of traveling the World after he received dreadful news about his health, the creative concept illustrates this adding a map and important icons. For example, London tower, bus, suitcase, cemetery, museums, airplane and main characters (Ambrose and her wife Zipper). Besides, the street names are some key words of this book that helps the reader to know what is the story about. King cool KC (handwriting font) is used for the book title and author name, which describes the new personality of Ambrose (playful, attractive and youthful). The chosen colours represent the three main countries in the story, London, Amsterdam and Zanzibar.

Chocolate Label

The main imagery was inspired by Pablo Lobato style. The foundation of this particular style is the use of gestalt principles (proximity, similarity, closure and symmetry). The protagonist for this special chocolate edition is Frida Kahlo, a mexican painter. She was recognized by her united eyebrow and big hairstyle with lots of flowers in her hair. Kahlo's work is remembered for its pain. passion, intense, and vibrant colours. "I'm not sick. I'm broken. But I'm happy to be alive as long as I can paint." Frida Kahlo

Cover Photo-V Magazine

V Magazine is an American Fashion Magazine published since 1999. It is seasonally printed and highlights trends in fashion, film, music and art. The theme for this magazine cover is Art Butterfly, which represents the concept of transformation of the new fashion trends, fall 2013. The colour palette is based on fall hues such as green, blue, pink, and cream. Values like black and white have been used to create contrast. Also, opacity creates an illusion effect on the graphics and gives the feeling of a canvas painting. Technical elements: f/2.8, 1/40, Auto, ISO 200. Model: Adriana Cruz.
